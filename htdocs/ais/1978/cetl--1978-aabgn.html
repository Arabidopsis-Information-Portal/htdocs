RELATION BETWEEN THE VIABILITY AND FERTILITY COMPONENTS OF THE ADAPTIVE VALUE OF GENOTYPES IN ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA (L.) HEYNH

I. Cetl and Marie Porubova-Svobodova

Department of Genetics, Institute of Microbiology, Genetics, and Biophysics Science Faculty J.E. Purkyne University Brno, CSSR

The procedure of measuring adaptive values of A. thaliana genotypes in experimental populations carrying two alleles of the same locus and three corresponding genotypes (Svobodova and Cetl, 1977) enabled us to focus our attention on the inter relation of the two components of the adaptive value. For this purpose, indexes of relative viability (v1, v2) were put against those of relative fertility (f1, f2) in the two homozygotes, +/+ and m/m, respectively (Figure 1, open circles). At the same time, corresponding correlation coefficients (r) and the two regression coefficients (bf and bv) were calculated with their standard errors (s.e.). When analogous data of Cetl and Plchova, (1975) were included (black circles) the picture did not change. No statistically significant difference of the correlation and regression coefficients from O was found. Thus the viability and fertility components of the adaptive value seem to be independent in the material studied.

No similar data in literature are available except for those by Allard, Kahler and Clegg (1977) who found that viability and fertility selection operated in opposite direction (pg 16). Such a case would be proved by means of negative correlation a regression between viability and fertility indexes. A third type of relation between the two components of adaptive value when viabiity and fertility selection operate in the same direction and correlation and regression are positive would be theoretically possible next to the two preceding types.
