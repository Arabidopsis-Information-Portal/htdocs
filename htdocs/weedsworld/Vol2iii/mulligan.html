Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Genetics
Dept of Life Science, Nottingham University

Applications are invited for a 3 year post-doctoral research fellowship (RA1A) funded by the EU to study the genetics and molecular biology of chromosomal deletions and deficiencies in Arabidopsis and to assess the value of such mutations for genome mapping and gene cloning. The project will involve interactions with other European laboratories and with the Nottingham Arabidopsis Stock Centre.

Candidates should have a PhD in plant genetics or molecular biology. The starting salary is flexible and will depend on research experience.

Applicants should send a detailed CV, together with the names of two referees to Dr Bernard Mulligan, Department of Life Science, University of Nottingham, University Park, Nottingham NG7 2RD, UK.

Closing date for applications: 26 January 1996.
