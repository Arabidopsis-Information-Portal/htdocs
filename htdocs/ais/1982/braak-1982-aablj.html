REVERSE MUTANTS OF THE NITRATE REDUCTASE-DEFICIENT MUTANT B73

Fietje J. Braaksma and W.J. Feenstra

Department of Genetics, University of Groningen, Kerklaan 30, 9751 NN Haren, The Netherlands

After the isolation of reverse mutants of the nitrate reductase-deficient mutant B25 (Braaksma and Feenstra, 1975, 1979, 1982; Feenstra and Braaksma, 1976) we were interested whether also revertants of another nitrate reductase-deficient mutant, B73, could be isolated. Reverse mutants of B25 could be isolated as green plants on a nitrate medium since the mutant B25 forms small yellow plants on this medium. Among ca. 30.000 M 1 plants (after EMS-treatment of the seeds) no green seedlings could be found.

Testing 1000 M 2 progenies of B25, 7 independently arisen revertants were isolated, resulting in a reversion frequency as high as 1.2 x 10E-3. Since B73 also gives yellowish plants on the nitrate medium, revertants can likewise be isolated as green plants. After EMS-treatment of B73 seeds (40 mM, during 4 hrs., preimbibed seeds) M 2 seeds from 6 M 1 plants were usually harvested together and sown as one progeny. After screening for green plants on nitrate medium surprisingly ca. 90% of the 200 progenies shown contained one or more green plants. This indicates an extemely high reversion frequency. Reversions did only occur after mutagenic treatment with B73, without such treatment B73 has never given a revertant.

The revertants of B73 grew clearly better on the nitrate medium than B73 itself but worse than wild type. Some relatively well growing plants were crossed with B73 and selfed. Most seeds germinated poorly or not at all, however, 4 different crosses evertant x B73 could be tested and yielded F1's exhibiting the phenotype of ž73; the revertant character in these cases thus is recessive.
