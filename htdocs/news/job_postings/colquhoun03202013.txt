POSTDOCTORAL POSITION 

Institute for Plant Innovation, University of Florida, USA 

Delineating Plant Chemical Signals and Human Sensory Association 

Starting Date: Immediately 

Project duration: 2 years, renewable for up to 3 years 

Project Description: Research will focus on the identification and analysis 
of specific biochemical constituents of flowers and fruits that influence 
human sensory and affective states. Applicants should have knowledge of gas 
chromatography/mass spectrometry and liquid chromatography/mass 
spectrometry technologies, as they will be expected to use these 
technologies in their research efforts. The appropriate candidate will be 
responsible for developing ideas and experiments related to this project, 
collecting and analyzing data, preparing research manuscripts and giving 
research presentations. The candidate will complete his/her research on the 
UF main campus in Gainesville, FL, but may be required to travel on 
occasion to assist with data collection. 

Requirements: Applicants are required to have a Ph.D. degree in plant 
biochemistry, metabolomics, molecular biology, or related field. Preference 
will be given to applicants who are also trained broadly in plant science 
and genetics, and have documented capabilities in personnel management. 

Deadline: Applications will be accepted until the position is filled.
