Postdoctoral Researcher ­ Axtell Lab, Department of Biology and the Huck Institutes of the Life Sciences, 
Pennsylvania State University, University Park, PA  16802

A Postdoctoral position is available to study small RNAs in plants.  Researchers interested in leveraging both 
Arabidopsis and the moss Physcomitrella to understand the functions of deeply conserved and lineage-specific 
microRNAs and siRNAs are encouraged to apply.  Additional research topics include high-throughput discovery and 
quantification of plant small RNAs with an emphasis on technology development and bioinformatics.

Candidates must possess a Ph.D. in Biology or a related discipline.  Candidates with experience using Arabidopsis 
and/or Physcomitrella as model systems are especially encouraged to apply.

The Pennsylvania State University is located in State College, Pennsylvania, a beautiful and diverse city that 
combines the ease of living in a small community with the cultural and recreational diversity of a large university.  
State College is also located within 4 hours of metropolitan New York, Philadelphia and Washington D.C.

To apply, send a Curriculum vitae along with three letters of reference to mja18 [at] psu [dot] edu or by post to:
