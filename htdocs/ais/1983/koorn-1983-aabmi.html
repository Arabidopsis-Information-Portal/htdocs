LINKAGE MARKER STOCKS OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

M. Koornneef and C.J. Hanhart

Department of Genetics, Agricultural University, Generaal Foulkesweg 53, 6703 BM Wageningen, The Netherlands

Until recently the gene map of Arabidopsis thaliana was rather fragmentary (Kranz and Scheidemann, 1978). By applying trisomic analysis the correct relation between chromosome/chromosome arm and linkage groups could be established (Lee-Chen and Steinitz-Sears, 1967; Sears and Lee-Chen, 1970; Koornneef and Van der Veen, 1983).

In addition, the mapping of 76 different loci on the 5 chromosomes made Arabidopsis thaliana one of the few plant species with a well established gene map (Koornneef et al., 1983). It seems useful to use this rather accurate and internally consistent map as a starting point and framework for future genemapping experiments.

In order to do this a number of well-defined markers should be available with well-established linkage positions distributed over all chromosomes. This short communication lists a number of lines homozygous recessive for at least 2 markers at the same chromosome or for markers at different chromosomes (Table 1). These lines and a number of trisomics and telotrisomics are available on request from the author's address.

This small collection will also be sent to the AIS seed bank at Frankfurt and can there be obtained from A.R. Kranz. These markers can be easily recognized by their mutant phenotype. However, cp-2 and f ca may give some difficulties especially when a population segregates for other plant length and flowering time genes. All lines are in the background of the "erecta" mutant of the ecotype Landsberg unless stated otherwise.

For a detailed description of the markers and their linkage relations the reader is referred to Koornneef et al., 1983. The map positions of the markers of Table 1 are summarized in Figure 1. The trisomics are described in detail and compared with other trisomics by Koornneef and Van der Veen, 1983.
