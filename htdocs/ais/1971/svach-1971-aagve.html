Fixation of 14CO2 activity of carboxydimutase and the content of free sugars in two chlorophyll mutants of Arabidopsis thaliana (L.) HEYNH.

J. SVACHULOVA

(Institute of Experimental Botany, Department of Genetics, Czech. Acad. Sco., Praha 6, Czechoslovakia)

The rare of 14CO2 fixation was followed in lethal chlorophyll mutans, strain 1467 of the race Dijon and srain 42 of the race St 56, cultivated on agar with sucrose. Both mutants fixated less 14CO2 than the normal plants.

The mutant 1467 with a chlorophyll content of 30-50% of the normal plants and with a normal ratio of chlorophyll a: b fixated less 14CO2 than the mutant 42 with a chlorophyll content of 10-20% of the normal plants and with an extremely low content of chlorophyll b.

The distribution of 14C in the alcohol-soluble and alcohol-insoluble fraction in leaves was the same for both, the mutants and the normal plants. There were no differences in the activity of carboxydismutase. Similarly the 5 free sugars: raffinose, maltose, saccharose, glucose and fructose, determined chromatographically, were detected in the same quantitative relation in mutants and normal plants.
