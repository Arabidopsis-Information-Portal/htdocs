INFLUENCE OF PH ON THE ACTIVITY OF RIBOSOPHOSPHATEISOMERASE OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA LEAVES IN RACE ENKHEIM AND ITS MUTANTS, TRIPLEX AND 58/15

N.K. Bakayeva and M.A. Babadzhanova

V.I. Lenin Tajik State University, Dushanbe, 734016, USSR

In the active enzyme centre there are ionizing groups, taking part in a catalytic action. Studying the influence of pH on the enzyme activity, we can define these ionizing groups.

Ribosophosphateisomerase (RPI, C.P. 5.3.1.6) was purified to the homogenous condition according to our previous method (Bakayeva and Babadzhanova, 1982). The enzyme activity was defined by the carbosole method (Hurwitz et al., 1951) and the amount of protein by the method of Lowry et al. (1951). The reaction with specific inhibitors was carried out by the method described in the practical guide of Kochetov (1980). Figure 1 presents data on the influence of pH on the RPI activity. The maximum activity is observed when pH is 7.0.

Calculations in lgv/pH coordinates (Figure 1) show the bends when pH for RPI from leaves of race Enkheim is equal to 6.4 and 7.5, from leaves of mutant triplex 6.7 and 7.6, and from leaves of mutant 58/15 6.6 and 7.8. According to Edsalls table (Dixon and Webb, 1979) we found the enzyme to have two specific ionizing groups: either two aminogroups in *alpha*-cysteine and one aminogroup in (*alpha*-cysteine and one sulphydrogenous group.

To clear up the role of amino- and sulphydrogenous groups, the activity of RPI was determined in the presence of specific inhibitors, namely the acetic anhydride and pyridoxale-5-phosphate for aminogroups and parachlormercurybenzoate (PCMB) for sulphydrogenous groups.

According to our results, the activity of RPI from leaves of race Enkheim as well as of mutants triplex and 58/15 under the action of acetic anhydride and pyridoxale-5-phosphate, decreases by 38-40 percent, and under the action of PCMB it decreases by 41-42 percent. Under the combined action of acetic anhydride and PCMB, the activity of RPI decreases by 37-40 percent, i.e., intensification of inhibition cannot be observed.

Thus, two ionizing groups take part in a catalytic action, pH of which probably corresponds to either two aminogroups in *alpha*-cysteine or one amino- and one sulphydrogenous group. However, proceeding from the character of inhibition by PCMB and acetic anhydride we may suppose that other enzyme conformations exist when ionization is defined by the other ionizing groups.
