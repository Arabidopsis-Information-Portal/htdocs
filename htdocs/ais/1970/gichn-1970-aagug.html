Comparison of the mutagenic activity of N-methyl-N-nitrosourea, N.N'-dimethyl-N-nitrosourea, and N,N',N'-trimethylnitrosourea

T. GICHNER, J. VELEMINSKY and V. POKORNY

(Institute of Experimental Botany, Department of Genetics, Czech. Acad. Sci., Praha 6, Czechoslovakia)

In the paper of GICHNER and VELEMINSKY (1967) the mutagenic activity of different N-alkylnitrosoureas was compared. The tested compounds differed in their alkyl groups (methyl, ethyl etc.) whereas their acyl group was the same. An increasing number of C-atoms in the alkyl group caused - with certain exceptions - a decrase in the mutagenic effectiveness, whereas the mtuagenic efficiency was approximately the same. It seemed, therefore, to be interesting to compare the mutagenic effectivness and the efficiency of nitroso compounds, having the same alkylnitroso group, but diffeering in the group bound on the acyl moiety. For this purpose 3 compounds were chosen: N-methyl-N-nitroso-urea (MNH), N,N'-dimethyl-N-nitrosourea (DMNH) and N,N',N'-trimethyl-nitrosourea (TMNH). All three compounds are strong cancerogens in rats (DRUCKREY et al., 1967). The chosen nitrosoureas differ in their hydrolysis rates, especially in neutral and in alkaline solutions. MNH is the most unstable compound, TMNH the most stable one.

All three compounds are strong mutagens for Arabidopsis thaliana. The mutagenic effectiveness, expressed by the concentration in MM, needed to induce 50% segregating M1-siliquae differed significantly (Table). With the increasing number of methyls, bound to the NH2 group, the effectiveness decreased. Of several possible explanations for these differences, the different stabiities of the tested compound seems to be the most plausible. The mutagenic efficiency of the tested compounds, expressed by the degree of sterility accompanying 50% segregating M1 siliquae, did not significantly differ (Table).
