SUMMARY

The common formula for the probability calculus for possibilities of distribution of several mutations in the apices with the given number of initials was elaborated. Experimentally the excess of multimutated nucclei in comparison to the expected number was found. A possible explanation is based on the different mitotic activity of the cells in the time of mutagenic treatment. Experimental data indicate the existence of the diplontic drift influence between initial and temporal specific repair processes after the mutagenic treatment.

INTRODUCTION

Treating the seeds at mutation induction with regard to the mutation realization in the next generation we are interested in genetically effective initials in the embryo shoot apex in those seeds. Many authors dealt with manifestation of the apex organization in Arabidopsis, i.e. M 1 chimerism. The number and size of mutated sectors either by M 2 segregation analysis or directly by localization of mutations inside inflorescences were studied (Muller, 1965; Shevchenko et al., 1975; Relichova; 1977; Mednic and Usmanov, 1982). So it was stated in Arabidopsis that the number of genetically effective initials (GECN) which gave rise to sporogenic tissue of mature plant was small, most probably from 2 to 4 (Langridge, 1958; Muller, 1964; Ivanov, 1971; Relichova, 1977; Grinikh et al., 1979; Bork et al., 1987).

Under certain conditions of mutgenic treatment when higher mutation frequencies were reached, results showed a possibility of rise of multimutations since some M 1 offspring segregate in M 2 more than one mutation in Arabidopsis (Muller, 1965; Balkema, 1971). It was proved (Relichova, 1977) that the occurrence of more mutations in the embryo apex include both principally different ways of distribution of these mutations, i.e., either each of them in another initial cell or several mutations in the single cell nucleus, i.e., the real multimutation. Also a system of classification of possible ways of distribution was elaborated for several mutations in the shoot apex (Relichova and Cetl, 1972) where e.g. the distribution of two mutations in the apex with two initial cells can be {1/1} or {2/0}, while the second alternative represents the real double mutation.

This paper adds to the theoretical model of mutation distribution (Relichova, 1981) a common formula for the probability calculus of distribution possibilities of several mutations and verifies it experimentally in Arabidopsis.

MATERIAL AND METHODS

Dry Arabidopsis seeds (race S96) were treated with different concentrations of N-methyl-N-nitrosourea (MNU .04, .08, .12 mM during 24 hours at 25*degrees*C and then 3 hours washed in water). Progeny of five succeeded pods per individual M 1 plant were grown in M 2. In M 2 the occurrence of chlorophyll and morphological mutations were observed and in case of occurrence of more than one mutation in the M 2 family the stability of mutants was examined in M 3.

In any case it is necessary to bear in mind that the distribution of mutations in the initial cells are evaluated according to their derivatives, i.e., as mutations realized in the M 1 inflorescences. It is well known that the apex structure goes through numerous changes during the development from the seed embryo to the mature plant. Regardless of the mutation induction, those changes concern GECN, while at least two initials in the dormant seed (Grinikh et al., 1979) about five GECN reach to the rosette stadium (Shevchenko and Grinikh, 1985). Simultaneously the same group of initial cells in the embryo apex give rise both to the generative tissue of the main and secondary inflorescences, and to the somatic tissue of the stem leaves (Shevchenko et al, l1977). In the mutation induction the fate of those mutations is important (i.e., initial cells carrying mutuation) in the embryonic developmental process. Diverse DNA repair systems are involved there (Veleminsky and Gichner, 1978) as well as the diplontic drift or diplontic selection (Muller, 1963; Balkema, 1971). The mathematical model of fixation or loss of mutations, be it selectively neutral or under diplontic selection, elaborated by Klekowski and Kazarinova Fukshansky (1984) emphasize the stochastic apex growth. At validity of that model it is evident that we cannot trace back the primary number of initials and the mutant changes induced in them according to the occurrence of the mutated sectors. Nevertheless, we can say that the experimentally obtained data represent their minimal values. These facts should be considered with the evaluation of the experimental data.

A conspicuous difference between the two alternative distributions can be seen favouring multimutated nuclei. One of the possible explanations is offered itself supposed already by Balkema (1971): In the time of mutagenic treatment the initital cells can have different mitotic activity, and then be differently sensitive to the mutagenic treatment.

From the cell side the mutation induction favoured some of them. The fate of these initials can be estimated as late as after the final realization in the M 1 inflorescence (via M 2). The cases when only one genetically effective initial manifested itself in the M 1 apices (Table 1), testify to the influence of the diplontic drift or diplontic selection. According to rather insignificant reduction of GECN with increasing concentration we can judge, that primarily random selection resulting in the diplontic drift is involved which takes place in the developmental process of the apex carrying the mutation. To this further cases bear witness in which the mutated sector involves the substantial part of the apical meristem.

At the topographic tracing of the occurrence of several mutations in the apex one more interesting finding was registered. In many cases proved through M 3 analysis, one mutated sector carried still one more smaller mutated sector. In those cases repair processes could be involved taking place in some time after the first division of the treated initial cells.
