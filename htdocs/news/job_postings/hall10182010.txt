The Institute of Integrative Biology, formally the School of Biological 
Sciences, is directed at fundamental science objectives from molecules to 
whole organisms with outstanding expertise in genomics, proteomics, 
glycomics, host-parasite biology, evolutionary studies, plant and cell 
science. 

You should have an excellent research and publication record, preferably in 
the area of plant science, as you will be covering the teaching commitments 
of a Reader within the Institute who will be undertaking a BBSRC Research 
Development Fellowship. You will also have the opportunity to conduct 
independent research. The post is available to 30 September 2013.
