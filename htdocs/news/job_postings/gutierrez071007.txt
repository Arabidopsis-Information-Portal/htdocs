Three Post-doctoral positions available at Pontificia Universidad Católica
de Chile.

Department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology, Pontificia Universidad
Católica de Chile to work on three different PBCT funded projects.

Induction of defense and environmental stress genes in plants:
identification of genes and promoter sequences that are key for defense.

Identification and characterization of a borum transporter in citrus. 
Contact: Dr. Patricio Arce

For more details of each project please contact the corresponding
investigator.

Successful applicants should have a relevant PhD in Plant Biology, be highly
motivated individuals with strong communication skills and be capable of
working independently. Experience in some or all of the following
disciplines is desirable: genomics, genetics, molecular biology and
bioinformatics. 

The Department of Molecular Genetics and Microbiology (DGMM) has a highly
interactive group of plant scientists. The DGMM will be moving to newly
built facilities in August 2007. The DGMM is located in the Main Campus of
the P. Universidad Católica de Chile, in downtown Santiago, the capital and
main city in Chile.
