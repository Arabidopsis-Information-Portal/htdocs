Storage effect in gamma-irradiated Arabidopsis seeds

V.I. IVANOV, A.V. SANINA and H.A. TIMOFEEFF-RESSOVSKY

(Institute of Medical Radiology, Obninsk, USSR)

Experiments are in progress to determine the effect of room temperature storage of gamma-irradiated dormant seeds on survival, growth, development, fertility, and mutation rate in Arabidopsis thaliana (L.) HEYNH. race Enkheim. A summary of preliminary results of the root growth studies is presented below. The seedlings for root growth measurements were grown either in Petri dishes ("Keimwurzeltest" by MULLER, 1964), or in usual test tube culture by LANGRIDGE (1957).

In the first series of experiments (see Table 1) a single dose of irradiation was used, while the storage time was varied. A marked (storage time dependent) increase in irradiation induced root growth inhibition was observed.
