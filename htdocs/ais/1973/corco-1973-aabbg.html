Vol 10:

An arginine mutant

A. CORCOS

(Kedzie Laboratory, Department of Natural Science, Michigan State University, East Lansing, MI, U.S.A.)

Seeds of the variety Estland were treated with ethyl methanesulfonate (10 mM, 22*degrees*C) for 16 hours. Among the M2 seedlings, one nutritional mutant was rescued in a complete liquid medium, asexually propagated and tested on different solid media. It was soon discovered that this nutritional mutant was an amino-acid mutant which was later identified as an arginine mutant. It grows well in presence of arginine, citrulline, ornithine, glutamic acid and proline.
