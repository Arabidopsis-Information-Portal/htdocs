A MUCILAGE POLYSACCHARIDE SECRETED FROM TESTA OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

Nobuharu Goto

Department of Biology, Miyagi College of Education, Aoba-Yama, Sendai 980, Japan

Generally, the Arabidopsis seed is covered by a mucilage layer immediately after imbibition. This mucilage is secreted from a cylindrical structure which is situated on the central region of each testa cell (Koornneef, 1981; Goto, 1982).

The present paper describes some charactristics of the mucilage investigated with the gel and gas chromatographic analyses.

Seeds (Arabidopsis thaliana, wild line Sendai, 30 g) were stirred in 0.2% ammonium oxalate (300 ml) at room temperature for 1 h. After centrifugation (1.3 x 10 4 g, 20 min) the extract was poured into 5 volumes of ethanol, and centrifuged (2.3 x 10 3 g, 20 min). The precipitate was dissolved in water, followed by dialysis against running water. A white filmy material (0.56 g) was obtained after lyophilization. The mucilage sample was analyzed by gh phenol-sulfuric acid method for polysaccharides using rhamnose as a standard, and by the calbazole-sulfuric acid method and thin layer chromatography for uronic acids using galacturonic acid as a standard. The analysis and calculation of the water dissolved sample revealed that neutral sugars accounted for 29 mg in 100 mg dry mucilage.

The sample (10 mg) was dissolved in 10 ml Tris/HC1 buffer (0.1 M, pH 7.7) and applied to a column (2.5 x 35 cm) of Sepharose 4B. Elution (7 ml/h) was carried out but the ascending method with the Tris-HC1. Fraction of 3.2 ml was collected and the polysaccharide was asayed (Figure 1). The gel filtration profile revealed that the mucilage was composed of homogeneous polysacchride, being greater than 2 x 10 6 in molecular weight. Tubes 15 to 25 in the Sepharose 4B elution were combined and dialzyed against water. This sample was hydrolyzed with trifluoroacetic acid (final concentration was adjusted to 2 N) in a sealed tube at 120*degrees*C for 1 h. The hydrolysate was vacuum dried at 40*degrees*C and dissolved in water (1 ml).

The neutral sugars were converted into their corresponding alditol trifluoroacetates according to the method of IMANARI et al. (1969). Gas chromatography was carried out with a Hitachi 163 gas chromatograph equipped with a hydrogen flame ionization detector. The glass column (200 x 0.3 cm) was packed with 1.5% QF-1 on Chromosorb W (AW-DMCS) (60-80 mesh). The analysis was performed at 130*degrees*C at nitrogen flow rate of 30 ml/min. Table 1 shows the result of gas chromatographic analysis of neutral sugar composition. The mucilage polysaccharide consisted mainly of rhamnose although six other and one unknown monosaccharide were contained in lower ratios.

From these data the mucilage in the testa seems to be composed of a pectic substance, having mainly rhamnose as the neutral sugar.
