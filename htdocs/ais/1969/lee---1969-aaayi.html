The lack of pollinating ability in the Fragilis trisomic

Suzanne LEE-CHEN and LOTTI M.S. SEARS

(Department of Genetics, University of Missouri, Columbia, Missouri, U.S.A.)

Although Arabidopsis thaliana is an autogamous plant, the Fragilis trisomic does not set seeds without artificial pollination. This trisomic has two flower anomalies, either or both of which may be responsible for the failure of pollination: (1) Hetrostyly, the stigma protruding beyond the anthers. (2) Protogyny, the female gametophyte maturing a few days earlier than the male.
