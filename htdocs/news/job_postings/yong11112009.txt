Bioinformatics Postdoctoral Position

 

Department of Plant Sciences, University of California, Davis, CA, USA

 

A bioinformatics postdoctoral position is immediately available in the Department of Plant Science at the University of California, Davis,

to work as part of the multiple-laboratory team on development of a high-resolution radiation-hybrid map for wheat D genome.

The research project is funded by the Plant Genome Research Program of the National Sciences Foundation (NSF) for 3 years.

Specifically, the incumbent will work with the bioinformatics team that manages the GrainGenes database ( http://wheat.pw.usda.gov/).

The incumbent will be physically located at USDA-ARS, Western Regional Research Center, Albany, CA, and responsible for

developing the project database and bioinformatics tools facilitating high throughput mapping in polyploid wheat and comparative genomics.

We are looking for a highly motivated individual with demonstrated ability to carry our research in wheat genomics.

The successful applicant will possess demonstrated skills in written and oral communication. Salary is commensurate

with experience and the benefits are covered under s policy (http://gradstudies.ucdavis.edu/postdocs/benefits.html).

Applicants interested in this position should send a single PDF file containing (1) a cover letter summarizing research interests,

(2) curriculum vitae, and (3) names and contact information for 3 references to Yong Q. Gu yqgu@ucdavis.edu or Ming-Cheng Luo mcluo@ucdavis.edu.
