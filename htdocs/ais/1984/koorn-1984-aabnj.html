THE LOCALIZATION OF TWO ABA INSENSITIVE GENES

M. Koornneef and C.J. Hanhart

Department of Genetics, Agricultural University, Generaal Foulkesweg 53, 6703 BM Wageningen, The Netherlands

INTRODUCTION

Recently a map of Arabidopsis thaliana was published locating 76 genes at distinct positions on one of the five Arabidopsis chromosomes (Koornneef et al., 1983). Further linkage studies now concentrate on the mapping of genes specially of interest for detailed biochemical and physiological studies and on making the present map more accurate. In this report the linkage relations of two abscisic acid (ABA)-insensitivity genes abi-1 and abi-3 (Koornneef et al., 1984) are presented.

MATERIAL AND METHODS

The mutants used in the present study and the procedures to estimate recombination percentages from F 2 populations and F 3 *progeny tests are described in Koornneef et al. (1983). Final map distances were estimated by a computer program written by Dr. P Stam, based on the procedures described by Jensen and Jorgensen (1975).

When two markers segregated in both a similar population (mainly F 2) and phase as in previous experiments, the new data were combined with the previously obtained data and a new more accurate recombination percentage was estimated.

Because of the variation in ABA-sensitivity observed between individuals of the same genotype (Koornneef et al., 1984), unambiguous scoring for this character is not possible for every F 2 plant. Therefore ABA sensitivity was assayed on the behaviour of each of the F 2 plants progenies on 10 micro M ABA. For DV (abi-3 2), which is more sensitive to ABA than CIV (abi-3 1) and AII (abi-1 1), the sensitivity on both 3 and 10 micro M ABA was taken into account.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

For preliminary linkage tests, the ABA-sensitive mutants AII (abi-1) and CIV (abi-3) were crossed with an/an, pv/pv, gl-l/gl-l, cer-2/cer-2, ms-1/ms-1. The resulting F 2 was sown on 10 microM ABA. Among the insensitive progeny there was a shortage of cer-2/cer-2 plants in the AII F 2 and a shortage of gl-l/gl-l plants in the CIV F2 indicating the location of abi-l on chromosome 4 and of abi-3 on chromosome 3. Detailed linkage data were obtained from the analysis of the F2's derived from cp-1/cp-1, cer-2/cer-2, ap-2/ap-2 x AII and from, hy-2/hy-2, gl-l/gl/l, tt-5/tt-5 x CIV respectively x DV (Table 1). The distribution over the different phenotypic classes did not differ significantly between CIV and DV (*chi*sq. = 16.3, df = 23, 0.8 P 0.9), confirming allelism of both mutants as was suggested before (Koornneef et al., 1984). The combined data of CIV and DV were used to estimate the recombination percentages that are shown in Table 2 for chromosome 3 together with the estimates for chromosome 4. The localization of abi-3 between hy-2 and gl-l looks clear.

The two recombinants abi-1/abi-1, cer-2/cer-2 (Table 1) were not conclusive for the localization of abi-1 between cer-2 and ap-2. However, the presence of an ap-2/ap-2 plant sensitive to ABA and the absence of a sensitive cp-l/cp-l plant makes the latter position of abi-l the most probable one. The revised maps of chromosome 3 and 4 (Figure 1) show only minor differences compared to the previous maps. The slightly changed position of cp-1 (15.9 instead of 20.3) on chromosome 4 derived from the new data obtained (Table 2) and from the deletion of the value cp-1-ag, 10.1 +/- 1.1 (Koornneef et al., 1983, Table V) from the final estimate of the map distances. This value was omitted because of its significant *chi*sq. value, when compared to the ultimate estimate of this distance (*chi*sq. = 6.3 before omitting it from the data file).
