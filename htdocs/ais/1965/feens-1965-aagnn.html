AIS Vol 02:

An emasculation technique

W. J. FEENSTRA

(Department of Genetics, Agricultural University, Wageningen, The Netherlands)

Emasculation is carried out under a sterio-microscope (10x) by means of a small pipette connected to a water jet pump. The pipette is drawn from a piece of thin-walled glass tubing, the entrance should be wide enough to let the largest anthers pass, but otherwise the tube should be as small as possible, to inflict as little damage upon the flowers as possible. A hypodermic needle, from which the pointed end has been cut off, will also act. The air flow is controlled by a pedally operated valve.
