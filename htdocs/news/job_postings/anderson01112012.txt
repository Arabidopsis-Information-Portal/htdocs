A Research Technologist position is available at The Pennsylvania State University beginning 
in January 2012. The successful candidate will perform research and organizational 
activities in the laboratory of Dr. Charles T. Anderson, Assistant Professor of Biology. 
The Anderson Lab uses molecular genetics, cell biology, and advanced in vivo microscopy to 
study plant cell wall biosynthesis and dynamics, with the goal of informing efforts to 
produce environmentally sustainable food, materials, and energy. This job will be filled as 
a level 1 or level 2, depending on the successful candidate's education and experience. 
Typical requirements for level 1 are a Bachelor's of Science degree or higher in plant 
biology, cell and molecular biology, or closely related field plus one year of related 
experience or an equivalent combination of education and experience. Additional experience 
and/or education and competencies are required for higher-level jobs. Hands-on laboratory 
experience is preferred, with expertise in several of the following techniques: molecular 
cloning, protein biochemistry, plant breeding and transformation, microscopy, and image 
analysis. The successful applicant must have the ability to plan, execute, interpret, 
summarize, and troubleshoot independent research and collaborate effectively with members 
of a research team. Excellent verbal and written English communication skills are required. 
Include a cover letter that summarizes relevant experience and reasons for interest in the 
job, along with a CV that includes contact information for three references (name, position 
title, mailing address, telephone number, and e-mail address). This is a fixed-term appointment 
funded for one year from the date of hire with the possibility of re-funding. Applicants should 
apply electronically to Job # 35724 at www.psu.jobs. Penn State is committed to affirmative 
action, equal opportunity and the diversity of its workforce.
