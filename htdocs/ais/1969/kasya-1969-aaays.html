Two allelic chlorophyll mutations of Arabidopsis thaliana

A.G. KASYANENKO

(Institute of Plant Physiology and Biophysics, Tajik Academy of Sciences, Dushanbe, USSR)

The new powerful mutagens induce a very wide spectrum of chlorophyll mutations. Of special interest are allelic mutations. Genetic analyses of viable chlorophyll mutations induced in our laboratory by gamma-rays and EMS and of mutations kindly supplied by Prof. Robbelen revealed that our mutation viridoalbina 40/3, induced by irradiation of seed (40 kR), is allelic to Robbelen's mutation "weiss ausbleichend, V-155/2. The F1 hybrids are all alike V-155/2. The F2 segregate monofactorially. Reciprocal crosses give the same results.

The responses of these allelic mutations to environmental conditions, however, are very different. In the beginning of the development they are phenotypically alike, later viridoalbina 40/3 grows very slowly. In the greenhouse, during the autumn-winter season, plants of V-155/2 are almost fully green, while viridoalbina 40/3 is green, white, small and weak. In the spring-season in the greenhouse they are phenotypically alike, but differ in size.
