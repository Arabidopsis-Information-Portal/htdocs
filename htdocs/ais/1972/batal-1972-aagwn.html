On the nature of pigment mutations in Arabidopsis thaliana (L). HEYNH

R.B. BATSLOW, K.V. KVITKO, and P.D. USMANOV

(Institute of Plant Physiology and Biophysics, Leningrad State University, Dushanbe, USSR)

The genetic nature of several pigment mutant forms was studied by crossing them with the markers gl1an (glabra = absence of hairs on the rosette leaves - 3rd linkage group, angulosa = narrow, stretched leaf - 1st linkage group; REDEI, 1955). The Table 1 shows that mutant strains ch(B) and "morphological", induced from ch(B), differ from the other forms by their heterozygosis in respect to lethal factors, which display in the appearace of vana type embryonic lethal (MULLER, 1963) in 2/3 of the posterity; this indicates to us that these mutations may be of the chromosome type. Our conclusion is confirmed statistically by analysis of the genotypic ratios inside the hybrid posterity in F2 and for gl+/gl- and an+/an- at the probability ratios 3:1 using the chi square method.

Table 1: Characteristics of the investigated pigment mutants

The mutants described in Table 2 may be divided into two groups: (1) mutants 24/45 "morphological", and a half of families from crossings of the mutant ch(B) show declination from segregation 15:1, displayed in the high number of recessives and (2) 117/2, spurio maculata, and another half of families from ch(B), having no such declination. Mutants 117/2 and spurio maculata behave like true gene mutations. Thus it is assumed, that the mutants "morphological" and 24/25 belong to the chromosome type with lethal effects in chromosome 1 for 24/25 or chromosomes 1 and 3 for the "morphological" mutant.

Double character of behavior of the mutants ch(B) in crossings is, probably, explained by different quality of gametes, formed in the process of meiosis (the change of the reciprocal translocation type is possible), as a result of that half of hybrid families from this crossing behaves in some cases as the posterity of gene mutation, in other cases as the posterity determined by the chromosome change.

More detailed description of the results of the genetic analysis is presented in the publication of BATALOV and KVITKO (1971)
