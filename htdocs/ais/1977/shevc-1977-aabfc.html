APPLICATION OF THE TECHNIQUE OF SOMATIC TISSUE CULTURE TO THE STUDY OF PLANT DEVELOPMENT IN ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

V.V. Shevchenko, L.I. Grinikh, V.A. Avetisov

N.K. Koltzov Institute of Developmental Biology K.A. Timiryazev Institute of Plant Physiology Moscow, USSR

In Arabidopsis thaliana it is possible to reveal mutations in generative tissues of X1-plants before seed maturing (Muller's embryo test). Therefore, using the technique of somatic tissue culture we can state whether the same group of initial cells of the seed or different ones form generative and vegetative tissues.

A. thaliana seeds (race En) were irradiated with 50 kr gamma-rays, then plants were grown at 24 +/- 2*degrees*C. Plants with chlorophyll deficiency mutations (with normal or somewhat underdeveloped embryos) were selected and their cauline leaves were cut to get calluses and to regenerate plants from them. The technique used was described by Avetisov et al. (1976)

Seven X1-plants carrying mutations were studied (Table 1).

There were no mutations in the progeny of regenerated plants obtained from the leaves of the two chimeric plants Y-1 and XI-1 but in the progeny of the other five plants the mutations were observed, which were phenotypically the same as donor plants.

By using test crosses it was proved that the mutations in donor plants and in the progeny of the regenerants obtained from their leaves were identical (Shevchenko et al., 1977).

Thus, by the direct method we showed that the same group of initial cells in apical meristem of the Arabidopsis thaliana seed produced both generative (main and side inflorescences) and somatic (cauline leaves) tissues. These data are in agreement with those of Hirono and Redei (1965) which were obtained after irradiation of the seeds of chlorina/+ heterozygotes.
