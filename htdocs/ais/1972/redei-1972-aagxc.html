Obligate photoorganotrophy in submerged culture

G.P. REDEI

(Department of Agronomy, University of Missouri, Columbia, Missouri)

Last year we reported (REDEI and PERRY, 1971) that Arabidopsis can be very successfully cultured in shaken or aeriated submerged liquid medium supplemented with glucose. Recent experiments have shown (Table 1) that an appropriate carbohydrate source is indispensable for growth even under high light intensity. The carbohydrate cannot be replaced by acetate, and there is an obligate requirement for light even on sugar containing media.
