Evaluation of the penetration and incorporation into DNA of triated thymidine in Arabidopsis seeds

M. JACOBS and S. BONOTTO

(Laboratory for Genetics of Higher Plants, Free University of Brussels, and Section of Photobiology-Agronomy, Department of Radiobiology, C.E.N., Mol, Belgium)

There is great interest to know the quantitative evolution of the incorporation pattern of tritiated thymidine into the DNA of Arabidopsis meristems, among others to interpret sensitivity of embryo seeds to mutagens and to determine the length of the mitotic cycle and its main phases. Some preliminary results are here reported.

Seeds of Arabidopsis, Wil-2, were immersed in a solution of 3H-thymidine (3 curies/mM: Radiochemical Centre, Amersham) at a concentration of 33 micro c/ml during increasing times up to 48 hours and placed in an environment of continuous light at a temperature of 24 +/- 1.5*degrees*C. After washing, the extraction of acido-soluble fractions and acido-insoluble fractions has been operated and the amount of radioactivity measured in a scintillation spectrometer. The results are presented in the figure. These data indicate that after soaking of seeds during 25 hours, penetration and incorporation suddenly increase and reach a maximum +/- 40 hours after sowing. The peak would represent the first wave of cells that go through the first metaphase. These results correspond approximately to those reported by ROBBELEN (1965) who mentioned the beginning of DNA synthesis 30-35 hrs and the onset of mitosis later than 36 hours after the soaking of the seeds.
