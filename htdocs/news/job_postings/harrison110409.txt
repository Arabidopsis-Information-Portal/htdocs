Limit of tenure: research grant funding available for 3 years only.

Applications are invited for a three-year postdoctoral research associate position in Dr Jill Harrison's group in the Department of Plant Sciences: Oxford and Cambridge.

Ideally, the successful applicant will be available to start in January 2010 when Dr Harrison relocates to Cambridge. They will join a team working on a five-year project funded by the Gatsby Charitable Foundation.

The main goal of the project is to elucidate the genetic basis of major innovations in body plan during plant evolution, and the first steps will be to characterise relevant aspects of development using a combination of genetic, imaging and modelling tools.

Further enquiries should be addressed to Dr Jill Harrison at jill.harrison@plants.ox.ac.uk .

Applications, with CV and cover sheet PD18 - parts I and III only, should be submitted to Mrs Catherine Butler, Department of Plant Sciences, Downing Street, Cambridge, CB2 3EA or email cek31@cam.ac.uk.

The University values diversity and is committed to equality of opportunity. The University has a responsibility to ensure that all employees are eligible to live and work in the UK.
