mi RFLP Hybridization-Pattern Table


The table given below gives the hybridization patterns of the mi RFLP markers across the ecotypes Columbia (Col), Landsberg erecta (Le), Rschew (RS) and Wassilewskija (WS) (Seeds obtained from Lehle Seeds). These data were obtained from the initial screening of clones for restriction fragment length polymorphisms, using the enzymes BamHI (B), DraI (D), EcoRI (EI), EcoRV (EV), and HindIII (H). Enzymes subsequently used in mapping are marked with an asterisk (*).

These data relate to the mi RFLP marker map table.

The hybridization pattern observed with each marker clone/restriction digest combination in the Columbia ecotype was arbitrarily assigned the Roman numeral designation "I", and patterns observed in other ecotypes were assigned the same designation if no polymorphism was detected, or consecutively higher numbers with each unique polymorphic pattern. Absence of hybridizing bands is denoted with "-".

Although some markers were later designated "a", and "b", to distinguish two independently segregating bands hybridizing to the same clone (i.e., mi79, mi291 and mi423), data was insufficient to make such a distinction in this table.

The raw-data file in tab-delimited format, which should be importable into most spreadsheet programs on most platforms, is available.

Quality of the data
These hybridizations were carried out only once, usually on filters that had been rehybridized repeatedly. This data is made available to serve as a guide for those whose work involves ecotypes aside from Columbia and Landsberg erecta. Should you obtain data at odds with this table, at least two potential sources for discrepancy exist. 1) Electrophoresis conditions may differ; we used field inversion gel electrophoresis to improve separation of larger fragments. 2) Our data may contain errors, although the scorings themselves were double-checked.

Reference
Y.G. Liu., N. Mitsukawa, C. Lister, C. Dean, & R.F. Whittier (1996) Isolation and mapping of a new set of 129 RFLP markers in Arabidopsis thaliana using recombinant inbred lines. The Plant Journal, 10, 733-736.
