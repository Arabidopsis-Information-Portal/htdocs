THE EFFECTS OF 2,4-DICHLOROPHENOXYACETIC ACID ON THE ROSETTE DEVELOPMENT OF MUTANT Dwf AND WILD TYPE OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA L.

J.I. Mirza

Institute of Pure and Applied Biology, Bahauddin-Zakarija University, Multan, Pakistan

The plant growth regulator 2, 4-dichlorophenoxyacetic acid (2, 4-D) is an important selective herbicide. As a synthetic counterpart of indole-3-acetic acid (IAA), 2, 4-D has widely been used in biological assays. In young seedlings of Phaseolus vulgaris, sublethal concentration of 2, 4-D induced generation of bizarre leaf form, including funnel shaped and tubular shaped leaves (Pereira and Dale, 1982). In this paper, the effects of 2, 4-D on the rosette development of wild type (WT) and an auxin-resistant dwarf mutant of Arabidopsis thaliana are compared and described.

The mutant Dwf was induced and isolated in Arabidopsis thaliana L., ecotype Landsberg, erecta mutant (Maher and Martindale, 1980; Mirza and Maher, 1980). Preliminary details of the mutant Dwf have been given by Mirza and Maher (1985). The mutation in the homozygous state (Dwf/Dwf) is lethal beyond seedling stage, but the progeny of heterozygous plants (Dwf/dwf+) segregate to give dwarf and wild type seedlings in a 3:1 ratio. Compared to WT, Dwf seedling roots exhibit 2000-fold increased resistance to 2, 4-D.

Seedlings of Dwf progeny were germinated and grown (at 25 +/- 1*degree*C) in Petri dishes containing perlite which was moistened with the mineral medium described by Oostindier-Braaksma and Feenstra (1973) and supplemented with 1 micro M 2, 4-D. During incubation, constant illumination was provided from white fluorescent tubes with photon flux density of 150 micro C mE-2sE-1. Seedlings were also grown in control dishes without 2, 4-D.

After 10 days incubation, the rosette growth of both Dwf and WT seedlings was reduced in dishes containing 2, 4-D. At this stage both types of seedlings seemed to be equally affected. However, after 15 days incubation, the rosette growth of Dwf seedlings was apparently much more affected compared to WT seedlings (Figure 1). In Dwf seedlings, rosettes showed extensive abnormal development and in most cases their leaves fused together completely in a girdle around the shoot apex forming a funnel-shaped or tubular-shaped structure leaving the apex at the bottom fo the cup-shaped development. On the other hand, WT seedlings did not form funnel-shaped or tubular-shaped structures, although their leaves were sometimes deformed.

The effects of 2, 4-D on the rosette development of Dwf are similar to those observed in Phaseolus vulgaris (Pereira and Dale, 1982). However, in view of the very high resistance of Dwf roots to 2, 4-D, it is difficult to explain why rosette leaves of Dwf seedlings are more affected by 2, 4-D than those of WT seedlings.

The effects of 2, 4-D on the rosette development of an auxin-resistant mutant (Dwf) and wild type WT seedlings of Arabidopsis thaliana were observed. The rosette leaves of Dwf seedlings grown on perlite with 1 micro M 2, 4-D, were fused completely around the shoot apex to form cup-shaped or tubular shaped structures. In contrast, wild type seedlings did not form such abnormal stuctures although their leaves were sometimes deformed.
