COMPARATIVE INVESTIGATION OF RIBOSEPHOSPHATE-ISOMERASE FROM ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA RACE ENKHEIM AND ITS MUTANT TRIPLEX

N.P. Bakaeva and M.A. Babadjanova

Institute of Plant Physiology Lenin Tajik State University, 74003 Dushanbe, USSR

Comparative investigations on the activity of one of the photosynthetic enzymes, ribosephosphate-isomerase (RPI, C.E. 5.3.1.6) were carried out on the leaves of Arabidopsis thaliana, race Enkheim, and its mutant triplex. RPI accelerates the reaction of the isomerisation of ribosephosphate (R-5-P) into ribulose-5-phosphate (Ru-5-P), participating in the regeneration of the acceptor CO 2 at the ribulose-1.5-diphosphate photosynthesis.

The spontaneous mutant has an inceased number of pods - it forms 3 to 5 on one fruitstalk - which defined its name as highly productive or triplex. The gene analysis states that the mutation involves the fifth linkage group.

The plants were grown under uniform conditions. The RPI enzyme preparations from the original race and mutant leaves were isolated simultaneously. To obtain RPI enzyme preparations 100g of each plant leaves were taken. All the procedures of enzyme isolation were carried out in cold at the temperatures of 0-4*degrees*C. The amount of protein was defined by Lowry method (Lowry et al., 1951) and RPI activity by modified Axelrod-Yang method (Axelrod and Yang, 1954). Purification procedures were selected separately for the original race and its mutant and were thoroughly controlled and analyzed.

In this paper the final data on the purification of enzyme RPI preparations from leaves of Arabidopsis thaliana, race Enkheim and mutant triplex are presented.

Table 1 shows the procedure and results of RPI purification from the leaves of the original race Enkheim and mutant triplex. The data obtained show that the yield of enzyme protein from leaves of Enkheim is 3% and of triplex 4%. After all the purification procedures the activity of RPI from the leaves of the original race Enkheim increases 9-times and the enzyme activity from the triplex leaves 11-times. The comparison with the original form shows that in protein amount and specific activity triplex is 1.5-2-times higher than Enkheim.

The differences in RPI activity from the leaves of original race Enkheim and its mutant triplex are most clearly shown at the estimation of the total fraction activity.

Thus, the data obtained permit us to make a conclusion on the higher RPI activity in the triplex leaves which may be one of the reasons for stimulating the productivity of this mutant.
