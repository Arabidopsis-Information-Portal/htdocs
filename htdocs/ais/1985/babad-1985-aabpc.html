TEMPERATURE INFLUENCE ON THE ACTIVITY OF RIBOSOPHOSPHATE ISOMERASE (RPhI) FROM ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA LEAVES, RACE ENKHEIM, AND ITS MUTANT TRIPLEX

M.A. Babadzhanova and N.P. Bakaeva

V.I. Lenin Tajik State University, 734016 Dushanbe, USSR

RPhI enzyme (RPhI, NF 5.3.1.6.) in Arabidopsis thaliana leaves of wild type Enkheim and its mutants triplex and 58/15 is of different activity (Babadzhanova and Bakaeva, 1980). Enzyme from triplex mutant leaves shows the highest activity. The influence of temperature from 15*degrees*C up to 60*degrees*C on the native form of enzyme and its subunits has been studied in order to explain the highest activity of RPhI mutants.

The enzyme cleaning was done according to the method, described earlier (Bakaeva and Babadzhanova, 1982). RPhI activity was determined by the carbazole method (Hurwitz et al., 1956. The protein content was determined by the method of Lowry et al. (1951).

Figure 1 represents data which show that RPhI of wild type leaves and of mutant triplex has maximum activity at the temperature of 50*degrees*C-60*degrees*C, but RPhI of mutant 58/15 at the temperature of 45*degrees*-50*degrees*C. Subunits of wild type and triplex enzyme have maximum activity at the temperature of 45*degrees*-60*degrees*C, but enzyme of mutant triplex at the temperature of 45*degrees-55*degrees*C. Apparently, enzyme of mutant 58/15 distinguishes by the high sensitivity to high temperatures. representing data on the temperature influence on RPhI activity in Arrhenius-coordinates, we obtained the lines with a bend at about 35*degrees*C (Figure 2).

The presence of a bend point in plots is apparently explained by the fact that the energy of the enzyme activity, conditioned by variations of the enzyme molecular conformation, vary for different objects and different temperatures. To test this supposition we calculated temperature coefficients Q, reactions and the energy of enzyme activity E act. for low and high temperature regions.

As it is seen from the Table 1, there is a regularity for all three objects; the values Q 10 and E act. are less in the high temperature region than in the low temperature one. These variations point to the fact that the conformational RPhI molecular variations occur at the certain temperature. The E act. value of RPhI in the low temperature region is less in comparison with the wild type. In the high temperature region mutant 58/15 has the least E act. value. RPhI subunits of three forms have larger values of enzyme activity energy and of the temperature coefficient both in the low and high temperature regions.
