CHLORATE RESISTANCE OF CELL CULTURES OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

H.J. Scholen and W.J. Feenstra

Department of Genetics, University of Groningen, Kerklaan 30, 9751 NN Haren, The Netherlands

Oostindier-Braaksma and Feenstra (1973) isolated mutants of a. thaliana which were blocked in the uptake or reduction of nitrate by selection of chlorate-resistant seedings in M2's. In our studies we try to develop a method for isolation of nitrate reductaseless mutants of A. thaliana using the technique of tissue culture. Our aim is to investigate whether mutants can be found which differ from those that could be isolated as intact plants.

In order to establish the appropriate chlorate concentrations, callus cultures derived from anthers of the mutant type "erecta" of the ecotype "Landsberg" of A. thaliana (L.) Heynh. and a nitrate reductaseless mutant (B25; Oostindier-Braaksma and Feenstra, 1974) were used to inoculate suspension cultures (the ploidy-level was not determined). Media, culture conditions and plating technique were essentially as described by Negrutiu et al. (1975). From callus cultures of wildtype a cell line (A28) was isolated showing a vigorous growth on solid media. In suspension culture this line forms aggregates of uniform size (about 50 cells). When A28 is plated on solid media bright yellow colonies can be seen within a week. This makes A28 very suitable for plating experiments.

The nitrate reductaseless mutant B25 and line A28 were plated on solid media with increasing concentrations chlorate up to 2000 ppm. Our first results showed a growth inhibition for A28 with concentrations above 300 ppm. No colonies are formed when concentrations above 800 ppm are used. The growth of B25 is not affected with increasing concentrations chlorate above 300 ppm. The increase of dry-weight of wildtype and B25 calli showed the same pattern when these calli were incubated on solid media with increasing concentrations chlorate. The ratios of nitrate reductase activity in extracts of calli of wildtype and B25 grown on medium with nitrate and ammonium are comparable to that of young wildtype and B25 plants (18.2 and 19.8 rsp.) Muller and Grafe (1975) isolated nitrate reductase mutants of Nicotiana tabacum by selection for haploid chlorate resistant calli using chlorate concentrations above 500 ppm.

Our results suggest that with regard to the resistance to chlorate tissue cultures of A. thaliana show the same pattern as N. tabacum.

We conclude that chlorate-resistance can be employed for the selection of nitrate reductaseless cell cultures of Arabidopsis.
