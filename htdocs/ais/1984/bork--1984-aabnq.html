EARLY AND LATE BIOLOGICAL EFFECTS OF HEAVY IONS PRODUCED BY ACCELERATORS ON ARABIDOPSIS SEEDS

U.Bork and A.R. Kranz

Botanisches Institut, J.W. Goethe-Universitat Postfach 111932, D-6000 Frankfurt am Main 11

INTRODUCTION

The experiments with particles of high energy and atomic number (HZE) produced by powerful accelerators (esp. UNILAC of GSI, Darmstadt) have been continued (Koller-Lampert and Kranz, 1982, 1983) in order to improve qualification tests and to examine the biological effects of HZE-particles. These investigations are close connected with the Biostack-experiments, in which the biological effects of cosmic radiation (esp. HZE-particles) onto various biological objects are investigated (Bucker et al., 1978, 1981; Kranz and Bork, 1984).

METHODS AND MATERIALS

The seed material and methods for irradiation at UNILAC (GSI) as previously described (Koller-Lampert and Kranz, 1982; Kraft et al., (1980) have not been changed. The procedures for evaluation of the biological effects were improved and standardized (Kranz and Bork, 1984). Especially the radicle test (Muller, (1964) became better in handling the seeds and by measuring only the root length of 4 days old seedlings with a Leitz-Orthoplan microscope converted to micropositioning and data processing by Sirius I microcomputer. The number of seeds examined was 200 per particle-density.

RESULTS

In this paper the results of the irradiation-experiment with articles of 19.MeV/n Ne are presented and compared with data of earlier experiments.

Figure 1 shows the grmination-curves of the Ne-irradiated seeds and of the 18.MeV/n Kr-irradiation-experiment previously performed by Koller-Lampert and Kranz, 1983. Germination is only reduced in the Kr-irradiated seeds with increasing fluency. The radiation-effect by Ne is small and can only be seen at the very high flux-density of 1*10 9 p/cm 2.

In Figure 2 the influence of particle density on the survival of the seedlings can be seen very clearly, even in the Ne-experiment. The curve of this experiment shows a shoulder which had also been found in earlier experiments (Koller- Lampert and Kranz, 1983) with different HZE-particles. The shoulder in the Kr-cure is not as stronglyt marked as in the Ne-curve.

Figure 1 and 2 are clearly showing that there must be a strong influence of the charge and/or mass of the HZE particle onto the biological effects, because both irradiations had been performed with nearly the same energy per nucleon.

Figure 3 presents the summarized results of the Ne-experiment. Some more parameters had been evaluated. The particle-density seems to have an unequivocal influence onto the survival, the embryo-lethality and the radicle-length. Survival and radicle-length are reduced with increasing fluency of Ne. Embryo-lethality raises with increasing fluency. The correlation of particle-density with each of the two other parameters, i.e., total mutation-rate R and mutation frequency mc (dwarfs, multicaulus plus sublethals) all measured in M2, is not significant. But this may be based on a nonrepresentative sample size of M 1 and M 2 which is a prerequisite of successful mutant screening (Kranz, 1984). Also it is necessary to get more standardized culture conditions and to examine all samples of the different particle-densities simultaneously as far as possible. The spectrum of observed mutants must be defined more precisely.

SUMMARY AND CONCLUSION

The irradiation of seeds with 19.MeV/n Ne-particles shows a strong influence on the parameters survival, embryo-lethality and radicle length. Survival and radicle-length are decreasing and embryo-lethality raises with increasing particle fluency. A comparison of the results with data of an experiment with Kr (18.MeV/n) shows that the effects on germination and survival become more dramatic with increasing charge and mass of the projectile.

These results raise some more questions:

a) Does there exist a dependency of all examined parameters not only with increasing fluency but with inceasing charge, mass and energy of the projectiles?

b) What kind of dependency do we get for each parameter? Answers are expected from further experiments at the accelerators (Unilac and Bevalac) as well as from Biostack-experiments at the space shuttle.
