Help About TAIR's Submit Contig Details Forms


THIS PAGE PROVIDES FURTHER DETAILS AND HELP ABOUT THE FORMS YOU CAN USE TO PROVIDE PHYSICAL-MAP CONTIG, CLONE, AND PROBE DETAILS TO TAIR.

A shorter summary of the forms and links to them, may be found on the Intro to Submitting Physical-Map Detail to TAIR form.

This "About" page is linked from the forms in appropriate places. Once you've read the help you need, use the "Back" button of your browser to return to the form on which you were working.

The purpose of the forms is to allow the submission to TAIR of clone hybridization details from chromosome-walking and physical-mapping experiments in Arabidopsis. These data will then be made available to the community through the TAIR Web server. At the moment, we are able to display such data as tiling paths using TAIR database.

Please note, there is a separate form to use if you are providing BAC mapping-data.

You will need a forms-capable browser, such as Netscape, to complete these forms, which are all still under development. Please e-mail us at TAIR Curator with any suggestions you may have for improvements.

How do I enter data on Web forms?

For those of you unfamiliar with filling-in Web forms, there are five different ways in which data are entered. The first four rely on using the mouse only, the fifth requires you to type:
Radio buttons: Click me Or me I've been clicked
Simply click on the appropriate choice. Usually to select only one option out of two or more.

Checkboxes: Click me And me I've been checked
Click on a box to check or uncheck it. Can be used to select more than one option.

Buttons: Press to make something happen
(such as reset the form or to send information to us). Buttons may look different on your machine.

Select:
Click and hold the mouse over the box to select one of a list of pre-defined options.

Text-input. You just click in the box and then type into it
(sometimes the boxes are much bigger.)

Which Web forms do I need to complete?

In order to supply physical-map contig information to TAIR, you first fill in either a Submit Simple Contig Map Details Form or a Submit Complex Contig Map Details Form.

In both forms, you tell us who you are (in Section 1), the name of your contig (Section 2), and you then list the names of all the clones and probes used in your contig -- don't forget any end probes. If you have relatively simple data, which is best represented as a tiling path, complete a Submit Simple Contig... Form. If you have more detailed results, which include the physical relationships between the markers and so can be used to draw a more-detailed contig-map, fill in a Submit Complex Contig... Form. Please note, there is a separate form to use if you are providing BAC mapping-data.

Please fill in as much information as you can. Most sections are optional, although some text-input boxes (relating to your name, etc.) are labelled with "(Required)". These have to be completed in order for the form to work. Once you have completed one of these forms, and if you are supplying more detailed data, then please fill in an TAIR Submit Contig Probe Details Form for each probe in your contig, and an TAIR Submit Contig Clone Details Form for each clone hybridization used in your contig for which you have additional details. These may include such information as the size, type, map location, chimerism, etc. of individual clones or probes and Southern hybridization details.

Unless you are sending us a map in computer format (see next section), please FAX us a picture of your contig, with the clones and probes labelled and a scale to:
+1-415-723 7016.
Alternatively, please mail us a copy to:
David Flanders, Dept. of Genetics, Stanford University, Stanford, CA 94305-5120, USA.

Please note that the purpose of the FAX is solely to enable us to check that we are drawing your contig correctly. Please do not use the FAX as a substitute for filling in sections on these forms.

What do I do if I've already got mapping data in computer format?

If you already have some hybridization data in some sort of computer format, which you think may obviate the need to complete some of the sections of the forms or be otherwise informative, please e-mail us before starting on the forms, with details of the type of computer and program used, type of file, etc., at TAIR Curator. Such data might include text or spreadsheet (e.g., Excel) files of clone type and sizes or maps produced using drawing programs, such as Claris Draw.

SAVING FILES
Before sending files (instructions on how to do this follow), you will first need to save them, from within the program in which you you created them, in standard format. For example, save:
Word-processor (such as Word) documents as text only and/or rich text format (rtf);
Spreadsheet (Excel and the like) files as "tab-delimited text";
Graphic (MacDraw Pro, Corel Draw, etc) either as .pict or .pict2 (Mac) or preferably as .gif (PC, UNIX, Mac -- some programs or use GIFConverter).

Check that these standard files have all the information in them that the originals have. This is particularly important with graphics files, where things occasionally have a nasty habit of moving or changing colo(u)r. Then send us the files in both the original program's version and the version saved in standard format.

SENDING FILES
You can send files in three different ways: using ftp, Netscape, or Fetch. These are explained below. Fetch is the probably the best for Mac users, ftp for experts, and Netscape for non-experts not using Macs.

Whichever way you send files, please e-mail us to let us know that you've sent your files as we get no automatic notification of arrivals in our incoming directory.

Sending via ftp
If you know about such things, you can ftp the files to us at: genome-ftp.stanford.edu in the /incoming directory. Please create a sub-directory in there with your username as its name. Send text files as ASCII and other files as binary.

Sending using Netscape
You can send files using Netscape, although if you're using a Mac, the Fetch route, given below, is preferable. Simply connect to the following URL:
ftp://genome-ftp.stanford.edu/incoming/ and use the "Upload File..." command from the File menu to pick the files from your computer that you wish to send us.

Sending using Mac Fetch
If you're using a Mac, " Fetch" is an easy and reliable way to transfer files. Have a go and impress your friends. Simply fire up Fetch and from the "File" menu, select "New connection" and then:

In the "Host" box, type
        genome-ftp.stanford.edu
In the "User ID" box type
        anonymous
In the "Password" box, type
        your e-mail address (e.g., slave@poorlab.richuni.edu)
In the "Directory" box, type
        /incoming
Press the "OK" button
Note the use of lowercase. Once connected, "Create New Directory..." from Fetch's "Directories" menu giving the directory your e-mail username (the portion to the left of the "@" sign; no spaces) as its name. Once you've created and named the directory, double-click on it to open it (it will, for the moment, be empty). Then use the "Put File..." button to send us your file. If you have several files or folders to send us, use the "Put Folders and Files.." from Fetch's "Remote" menu.

When "Putting" files there are various options that come up in the "Format" section of the "Put File" window (this pops up once you've selected your file(s) with "Put"), send ("put") files in the following ways:
text (or .txt) files send as Text
.gif, .pict, .pict2 send as Raw Data
program files send as Mac Binary II, or BinHex

If you're confused or having problems, please e-mail us.

How do I enter mapping-data into the Submit Complex Contig Map Details Form?

If you have detailed mapping and hybridization data, which includes the physical relationship between markers, and you are able to draw a map to scale, you will first need to determine RELATIVE map postions of the clones and markers (probes), so we can re-draw your map. The crucial thing to do to enable you to do this is to locate the northern-most (left-most) end of the first CLONE in the contig and set this as 0 (zero) kb. Measure all distances from the right of this point as plus (this should be all points).

For example, you would have named the contig below (if on chromosome IV) "IV g1234 - mi50" and the left end of Clone B would be your zero point. (This is the same contig as shown above in the tiling path, but includes extra information -- specifically the sizes of the probes and clones and the distances between the markers.)

The positional data you enter on the "Submit Complex Contig Map Details to TAIR" form, would be:
    Probe NumberProbe Name Start (kb) End (kb)
    1g1234 50 70
    2g2468 130 170
    3RHS3 140 145
    4m456 220 240
    5mi50 260 265
    Clone Name Start (kb) End (kb) N Probe Number S Probe Number
    EG1A10 40 180 1 3
    yUP2B9 0 135 1 2
    CIC3C8 35 300 1 5
    EW4D7 120 245 2 4

What do I need to know about the PROBE form?

You should fill in a "Submit Contig PROBE details to TAIR" form after you have completed either a Submit Simple- or Submit Complex- Contig Map Details Form. In which, you will have listed the names of all the clones and probes used in your contig and give your contig a name. In the, "Submit Contig Probe Details to TAIR" you give further details of a specific probe from your contig. Please complete one of these forms for each probe used in your contig for which you have such extra information. Once you have done this, please fill in an TAIR Submit Contig Clone Details Form for each clone in your contig for which you have additional information.

Back to the top of the page

What do I need to know about the CLONE form?

You should only fill in a "Submit Contig CLONE details to TAIR" form after you have completed either a Submit Simple- or Submit Complex- Contig Map Details Form. In which, you will have listed the names of all the clones and probes used in your contig and give your contig a name. In the "Submit Contig Clone Details to AtDB" form, you give further details of a specific clone used in your contig. Please complete one of these forms for each clone in your contig for which you have such extra information. Once you have done this, please fill in an TAIR Submit Contig Probe Details Form for each probe in your contig, for which you have additional information, if you have not already done so.

Why do you ask so many questions?

The reason we ask you to fill in so many forms is two-fold. Most importantly for the users of these data -- your fellow reseachers -- to make as much chromosome-walking data available as possible. Second, to help us in our improvement of TAIR, particularly in our work to develop a more interactive user-interface to allow better access, searching and display of physical-map and other genomic information.
