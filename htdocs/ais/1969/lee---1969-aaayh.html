Vol 06:

A telotrisomic in Arabidopsis thaliana

Suzanne LEE-CHEN and LOTTI M.S. SEARS

(Department of Genetics, University of Missouri, Columbia, Missouri, U.S.A.)

Narrow-serrated (Ns) and Narrow-curved (Nc) trisomics were illustrated by STEINITZ-SEARS in 1963. They closely resemble each other in morphology, except the former has a more serrated leaf margin and a less curved petiole. However, studies showed Ns was triplex for both lu and tz, whereas Nc gave a positive result for tz but not lu. This would be expected if Nc is a secondary, tertiary or telosomic trisomic. Nc was found to be a telotrisomic by cytological examination. It is therefore established that tz and lu are on the different arms of this chromosome.
