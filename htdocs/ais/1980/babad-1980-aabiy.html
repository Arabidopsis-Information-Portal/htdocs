RIBOSEPHOSPHATEISOMERASE (RPI) FROM LEAVES OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA RACE ENKHEIM AND ITS MUTANTS, 58/15 AND TRIPLEX

M.A. Babadjanova and N.P. Bakaeva

Tajik State Lenin University, Dushanbe, USSR

The aim of our investigations has been a comparative study of the activity of the ferment RPI (E.C.5.3.1.6.) of the carboxylic phase of photosynthesis in leaves of Arabidopsis thaliana, original race Enkheim and its two mutants 58/15 and triplex. RPI catalyzes the isomerization reaction of ribose-5-phosphate into ribulose-5-phosphate participating in the regeneration of acceptor CO2 in photosynthesis of ribulose-1,5-diphosphate.

The plants were grown under similar conditions.  The light sensitive mutant, 58/15 was obtained by treating the seeds of the original race with ethylmethanesulphonate. Insufficient light as well as bright light resulted in less viable plants of the mutant. The plants did not accumulate biomass when the light was insufficient while the bright light yielded chlorophyll photodecomposition (Yakubowa et al., 1980). In the spontaneous mutant triplex, mutation led to an increase of the number of pods in one fruitstalk from 3 to 5. The increase of the fruitstalks, pods and the number of seeds allowed it to call the mutant high productive. It was etablished by genetic analysis, that the mutation in both genotypes concerns the 5th linkage group.

Ferment preparations of RPI from the original race and the mutants were isolated simultaneously. All the procedures of the RPI-isolation were carried out under cold temperature of 0 to 4*degrees*C. Leaves were rubbed in triple-volume 0.1M tris-HC1 buffer, pH 8.0, containing B M: MgC1 - 0.005, NaC1 - 0.2, reduced glutathion - 0.01. The homogenate was centrifuged with 18000 rpm for 1 hour. The protein quantity was determined in the supernatant by Lowry's method (Lowry et al., 1951) and the activity of RPI by Axelrod and Iang's modified method (Axelrod and Iang, 1954).

Table 1: Enzyme activities in A. thaliana, race Enkheim and the mutants 58/15 triplex.

From the data presented in the Table 1 it is evident, that the specific RPI-activity of mutant leaves 58/15 is slightly lower than that of the original race. RPI-activity of triplex mutant leaves is 25% higher than that of the original form Enkheim. Data of the RPI-activity per gramme of crude weight of leaves show, that the activity of both mutants is higher than that of the original form.

These results prove the increase of the protein content as well as of the RPI-ferment in the mutant leaves.
