Close-Up on Genetic Maps

Each genetic map is a graphical representation of a segment of a chromosome identified by the numbers at the top of the map. The location of the segment within the chromosome is illustrated by the red rectangle on the bar in the map menu:

The map shows markers on a horizontal chromosome bar. Visible markers appear as blue vertical lines and have names attached to them. Markers that are not visible at the current zoom level are indicated by gray vertical lines.

Three kinds of genetic maps are available:

  • Classical
  • RI
  • mi-RFLP
