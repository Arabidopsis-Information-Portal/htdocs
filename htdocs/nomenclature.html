This page presents links to guidelines for nomenclature, tools for the functional classification of gene products and other resources related to names/definitions of things.

Nomenclature Guidelines
How to name Arabidopsis genes identified by mutation (from David Meinke's lab)
Standards of nomenclature for genes identified by mutations that have been adopted by the Arabidopsis community and should be followed in publications and presentations. Includes information on how to reserve a mutant gene symbol. [Community Standards for Arabidopsis Genetics]

TAIR Guidelines for naming Arabidopsis genes
Suggested guidelines for naming Arabidopsis genes.

CPGN Guidelines for naming sequenced genes
Recommendations for naming sequenced genes from the ISPMB Commission for Plant Gene Nomenclature.

TIGR Annotation Guidelines
Guidelines used by TIGR for gene identification and naming. Explains the difference between a hypothetical and unknown protein.

MIPS similarity classes
Explains the difference between "strong similarity to" and "similarity to" with links to all genes that fall within each class.
ChromoDB Nomenclature Guidelines
Recommended nomenclature for Arabidopsis and Maize genes involved in chromatin remodeling.

Swiss-Pro links to nomenclature guidelines
Nomenclature guidelines for many classes of proteins as well as links to nomenclature documents for other organisms.

Gene Product Classification

Gene Ontology Consortium
All about the Gene Ontology Consortium and links to browse/search tools for the GO database. The GO has developed a structured controlled vocabulary to describe the cellular location(s), molecular process(s) and biological function(s) of genes in a variety of organisms. TAIR is collaborating with the GO to accomodate plant genes.

MIPS Classification of Arabidopsis Proteins
Link to MIPS functional classification of Arabidopsis proteins.

TIGR Gene Identification Table
Browseable index of TIGRs functional classification of Arabidopsis proteins and TIGRs version of the MIPS classification.
