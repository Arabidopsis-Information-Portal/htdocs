DEMONSTRATION OF NEW AND ADDITIONAL POPULATION SAMPLES AND MUTANT LINES OF THE AIS SEED BANK

A.R. Kranz

Fachbereich Biologie (Botanik), Universitat D-6000 Frankfurt/Main 1, Bundesrepublik Deutschland

During the 2nd International Symposium on Arabidopsis Research in September 1976 only a small number of selected but typical samples of the AIS-seed bank were demonstrated by slides and living material in the glasshouses at Frankfrut/Main (Kranz, 1976). Furthermore, in August 1978 a demonstration of new and additional population (wild races) and mutant (selected genotypes) samples was held during a workshop on Arabidopsis genetics associated with the 14th International Congress of Genetics at Moscow. This valuable material is based mainly on the well known collection of F. Laibach, G. Robbelen, D. Ratcliffe, and C. Gomez-Campo; further material was added by our group at Frankfurt since 1974.

The aim of this contribution is to present a complete documentation of this material in addition to the short reports of G. Robbelen (1965a) on the Laibach standard collection of natural races and of D. Burger (1971) on the morphological and seed colour mutants at Gottingen. In order to facilitate the orientation of the variable samples the following lists were computed on different key characters for the population material on the one hand and for the mutant lines on the other hand. The denotations and explanations are mostly given in agreement with those handed over to us by G. Robbelen. They are numbered and thus they can be extended on each level of classification. Leaf colour mutants were designated additionally by using the terminology of H. Lamprecht and following the proposals of A.J. Muller (1964) and of G. Robbelen (1965b). For the morphological mutant lines latin names were given in agreement with the main groups of the classification system of D. Burger (1971). Symbols and names of genes are those offered by G.P. Redei and A.D. McKelvie (1965) and were used only for those lines which were tested in genetic experiments. The data presented illustrate by example the principles of classification which are applied to data processing of the AIS-seed bank in total.

Further purpose of the following presentation is to inform everybody interested in this subject on its enormous variability, to enable researchers to select the material suited for their experiments, to eliminate misdesignations by this uniform and handsome classification of the seed samples, and last not least to stimulate the conservation of wild races of those regions of the northrn hemisphere not to be considered as well as the screening of unusual, new mutant types.

The AIS-seed bank is open to all informations on new material and to stock exchange. Small seed samples of the populations and the mutant lines, the older as well the new ones, can be received by request to the author.
