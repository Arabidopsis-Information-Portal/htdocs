MUTAGENIC EFFECTS OF MYCOTOXINS ON ARABIDOPSIS

Gregoria N. Acedo and G.P. Redei

Department of Agronomy, 117 Curtis Hall, University of Missouri Columbia, MO 65211

Various mycotoxins are potent mutagens and carcinogens for mammals as well as for some prokaryotes. Some of the fungi are also affected genetically by these fungal toxins whereas others are not susceptible to them (Ong, 1975). Aflatoxin B 1 is a clastogen for plants (Lilly, 1965; Reiss, 1971) but much less information is available regarding mutagenesis of mycotoxins in plant cells where these chemicals are released. Since various mycotoxins are ubiquitous on crops, a so far rather neglected source of "spontaneous" mutation may need attention.
