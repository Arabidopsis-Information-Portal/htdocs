Lecturer in Biology/Plant Biology 

University of Birmingham 
School of Biosciences 
12 months fixed term post 
Closing Date: 31st July 2013

Starting salary is normally in the range £37,382 to £44,607. With potential progression once in post to £50,186 a year.

The School of Bioscience aims to recruit a teaching-focused lecturing staff for a fixed term of 1 year commencing September 2013. The post will provide cover for a member of staff on a one year's research sabbatical, and is part of the University's commitment to deliver undergraduate education of the highest quality. The new lecturer will take roles in the school's delivery of the BSc in Biology with particular focus on Plant biology at both the environmental and molecular areas and may include some skills teaching (e.g. statistics). It would be desirable if the candidate had the academic experience to act as organiser for Modules, including plant molecular biology at final year level. There will be opportunities to contribute to enhancement of educational delivery across all academic staff in the school.

Role

You will deliver teaching and teaching-related activities in the School of Biosciences, with focus on Plant Biology. These will include module delivery, management of teaching-assistants/demonstrators, lectures and tutorials, assessment and educational enhancement. You will contribute to teaching-related administration.

Skills and Experience

You will have:

  • The ability to deliver and assess high quality teaching programmes across all years
  • Proven experience of research in Plant Biology
  • A strong commitment to educational enhancement
  • Experience of teaching-related management
  • A career ambition of delivering undergraduate education of the highest quality
