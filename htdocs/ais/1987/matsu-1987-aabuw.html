A REPEATED SEQUENCE IN THE GENOME OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

M. Matsui, N. Nomura and R. Ishizaki

Cancer Research Laboratory, Nippon Medical School at Sakuragi, 1-10-19 Uenosakuragi, Taitoku, Tokyo 110, Japan

INTRODUCTION

Arabidopsis thaliana has many ideal features as a model system for plant molecular biology, for its short life cycle, abundant mutant stocks (Kranz and Kirchheim, 1987), and especially its small genome size (70,000bp per haploid genome).

To understand the gene organization of this plant, we screened many genes from genomic DNA library of Arabidopsis thaliana using many probes. During the course of the work, we were able to clone the repeated sequence. In this report, we would like to describe the unique repeated sequence.

MATERIALS AND METHODS

A genomic DNA library of Arabidopsis thaliana cloned in a bacteriophage *lambda* was obtained from E.M. Meyerowitz (Chang and Meyerowitz, 1986). Sequence analysis was performed according to Sanger et al. (1977) on restriction fragments subcloned into M13 mp series vectors (Messing and Viera, 1982).

For Southern blot hybridization, seven micrograms of Arabidopsis thaliana race Columbia genomic DNAs were digested with appropriate restriction endonucleases, electrophoresed in agarose gel, and transferred to nylon membrane filters.

Probe DNAs were labelled with (*alpha*-32 P) dCTP by nicktranslation. Hybridization was carried out at 42*degrees*C for 15 hours in a solution containing 30% formamide, 6xSSC (lxSSC: 0.15M NaCl/0.015M sodium citrate), 10x Denhardt's solution (1x Denhardt's solution: 0.02% bovine serum albumin/0.02% Ficoll-400/0/02% polyvinyl-pyrrolidone), 0.5% sodium dodecyl sulfate (SDS). 0.5 mg/ml herring sperm DNA, 5x10 8-1x10 9 c.p.m./micro g DNA probe. Filters were washed three times in a solution containing 1xSSSC/0.1% SDS at 30*degrees*C and two times in 0.2xSSSC/0.2% SDS at 37*degrees*C.

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

From the nucleotide sequence of the repeat (Figure 1) it was found to be the tandem copies of 48 base pair repeating unit. We named this unit "Sac" for unique SacI restriction enzyme site in the unit.

Figure 1: The nucleotide sequence of repeats. Repeating units were aligned to achieve maximal homology.

The homology between each repeat and the consensus sequence is about 95% on the average. When this consensus sequence is aligned as Figure 2, the nucleotide sequence of the first half has homology (63%) the complementary strand of the latter half. The repeat of 48 base pairs (Sac) may be composed of inverted repeat of 24 base pairs.

Figure 2: Consensus sequence was divided into two parts and the complementary strand of the latter half was aligned to achieve maximal homology.

From the percentage of homology, it was suggested that inversion of the 24 base pairs occured first and then the resulting 48 base pair unit (Sac) repeated tandemly. It is uncertain whether this 24 base pair sequence is composed of much shorter repeating units. Using this repeated sequence as a probe, Southern blot hybridization was carried out in a relatiely low stingent condition (see Materials and Methods). The probe hybridized to many bands of different lengths in genomic DNA, when the genomic DNA was digested with BamHI or EcoRI (Figure 3). It was suggested that the repeated sequence was dispersed throughout the Arabidopsis thaliana genome. To examine whether this repeated sequence existed in other species, human DNA (placenta), rat DNA (Fisher rat liver), and mouse DNA (NIH3T3) were hybridized with the same probe in the same condition. In this experiment, however, no hybridization sign was observed (data not shown). This repeated sequence may only exist in plant genomes.

Some repeated sequences which are dispersed in the Arabidopsis thaliana genome have already been cloned by Pruitt (Pruitt and Meyerowitz, 1986). We do not know whether the repeated sequence presented here and the repeated sequence cloned by them are the same or not, because there is no sequence data for theirs. From the Southern blot hybridization patterns of theirs and ours, however, they were different. Recently, human repeated sequences (minisatellite), have come to be used as a probe for human DNA restriction fragment length polymorphism: RFLP (Jeffreys et al., 1985). The repeated sequence described here may also be a useful probe for RFLP of many strains of Arabidopsis thaliana.

SUMMARY

A repeated sequence was closed from the genomic library of Arabidopsis thaliana. It consists of tandem copies of 48 base pair repeating unit. Its unit is composed of two sets of distantly related 24 base pair sequences which are invertedly repeated within the unit. From Southern blot hybridization of the genomic DNA using this repeating units as a probe, it was suggested that this unit sequence was interspersed throughout the genome.
