PRIMARY PHOTOSYNTHETIC PROCESSES IN MUTANT V 76 OF ARABIDOPSIS THALIANA

M.M. Yakubowa, Z.A. Nazorova and G.A. Startsev

V.I. Lenin State University and Department of General Genetics of Cotton, Tajik Academy of Sciences, Dushanbe, USSR

This report deals with the primary photoinduced processes in the mutant V-76 of Arabidopsis thaliana (L.) Heynh. Control was wildtype Enkheim. Mutant V-76 has yellow leaves, small rosette and its height is two times less than that of the wildtype.

Table 1 shows that in mutant V-76 the length and the width of cells and quantity of chloroplasts in one cell is less than in the wildtype. Chlorophyll content per gramme of raw weight and per unit of area in the wildtype is 4-5 times more than in the mutant V-76 (Table 2). Low quantity of chlorophyll b is noticeable in the mtuant leaves; a/b ratio in the mutant is higher than in the control. Preliminary investigations show that 14 CO 2 fixation level per unit of leaf surface in the mutant is somewhat higher than in the control.

"Work" of the photosynthetic apparatus was characterized by following parameters: induction curves of delayed fluorescence and signals of electronic paramagnetic resonance (EPR) from cation radical of P-700. Analysis was done on intact leaves and on isolated chloroplasts. Induction curves of leaves and isolated chloroplasts of both objects have the same characteristic (Figure 1). Amplitude of the signal in the leaves of mutant V-76 is less. Fluorescence in the presence of 3-(3.4 dichlorophenyl) -1.1-dimethylurea (DCMU) is also less. Apparently it is connected with low content of pigments per unit of leaf surface. However, per unit of chlorophyll the mutant should have higher intensity of fluorescence. This was proved by experiments on isolated chloroplasts of subjects under study with equalized chlorophyll content. In this case DCMU inhibited fluorescence in the wildtype to a higher degree. It is known that fluorescence is connected with PS II reactions (Matorin, 1972; Hardt and Malkin, 1974). Thus the results of our experiments show that the mutant V-76 has a larger number of PS II reaction centres per unit of chlorophyll.

Figure 2 shows that, although different from wildtype, in the mutant V-76 the level of P-700 oxidation in light is practically the same as in darkness. It can be supposed that in the leaves of mutant intensive leakage of electrons from the electron transport chain in dark reactions, takes place, which is in accordance with the higher speed of CO2 fixation. In isolated chloroplasts, equalized in chlorophyll content, the light EPR signal is higher than darkness signal for both wildtype and the mutant. For the latter one, P-700 oxidation level in light is lower than for the wildtype which, apparently, is connected with larger number of PS I reaction centers per unit of chlorophyll.

Hence, under the effect of mutation a certain reorganization of photosynthetic apparatus has taken place in the mutant V-76, which has increased the number of PS I and PS II reaction centres and accordingly the number of electron transport chains per unit chlorophyll. Lower content of chlorophyll in the mutant V-76 is connected wih the eduction in the number of antenna pigments and first of all chlorophyll b.
