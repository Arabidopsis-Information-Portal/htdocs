Vol 01:

Nutrient recipes for selection and growing of biochemical mutants

M. JACOBS

  • I. The minimal medium is basically that of HOAGLAND-ARNON:
    • A. Major elements (in 1000 ml aqua bidest):
      • Ca (NO3)2 0.820 g
      • K NO3 0.505 g
      • Mg SO4 . 7 H2O 0.480 g
      • K H2 PO4 0.136 g
    • B. Iron: 13.05 g EDTA (Na-salt) in 134 ml of n K OH + 12.45 g Fe SO4. 7 H2O, adjust to 500 ml with aqua bidest. and shake for 24 hrs.
    • C. Minor elements:
      • Mn Cl2. 4 H2O 1.81 g
      • Zn SO4. 7 H2O 0.222 g
      • Cu SO4. 5 H2O 0.079 g
      • H3 Bo3 2.860 g
    • D. Molybdenum: Mo O3 150 mgrs in 10 ltr 1/10 n H2SO4.
    • Global formulation: 1000 ml A + 1 ml B + 1 ml C + 1 ml D.
  • II. Substrates:
    • 1. Agar 0.8% Difco Bacto-Agar
    • 2. Silica-gel oobtained through the passage of a sodium silicate solution in a glass column filled up with Amberlite IR 120 (Roehm and Haas, USA). Adjust to pH 6.2 with Na OH. the gel is very suitable after autoclaved sterilization.
    • 3. Quartz-sand poured in Soxlet thimbles for easy regulation of water supply and thes placed in sterile test tubes (ROBBELEN, unpubl.)
    • 4. Perlite or Vermiculite for plant culture.
  • III. Complete medium
    • A. Glucose: 0.5% in minimal medium
    • B. Casein hydrolysate: 0.01%
    • C. Yeast extract (if necessary): 0.01-0.1%
    • D. Purines and pyrimidines: see table 1
    • E. Vitamins: see table 2
    • F. Amino acids: as used by LANGRIDGE (1957b). Hydroxy proline may be adjoined at the rate of 10 mg/1000 ml medium.
All the supplements are dissolved in water except guanine. The complete medium is autoclaved (120*degrees*C, 15 min) or filtered through Millipore.
