Postdoctoral researcher position

A postdoc position is available at the Department of Microbiology and Cell 
Science of the University of Florida to work on epigenetic regulation of 
maize endosperm development. Applicants should have a PhD in plant 
molecular/cellular biology or related disciplines. The successful candidate 
will investigate epigenetic marks of genes expressed in maize endosperm 
cells. Familiarity with genomic data mining and sequence analyses is a plus. 
Salary will be commensurate with experience and qualifications. 

We are seeking an enthusiastic researcher interested in the molecular basis 
of plant cell differentiation and wants work in a vibrant research 
environment. Our lab is equipped with excellent microscopy and histology 
instruments. 



How to apply: 

Applicants should send a statement of research experience and interests, CV, 
and contact details of three references to Dr. Byung-Ho Kang, 
(bkang@ufl.eduor Microbiology and Cell Science, University of Florida, 
Gainesville, FL 
32611). Applications will be reviewed until the position is filled. Position 
starting date will be 15 March 2011, or shortly thereafter.
