The DNA probes and PCR markers used in this study are ordered along the base line. All the markers indicated were tested on the CIC YAC library. The yUP library was screened only with markers adjacent to gaps, in order to bridge the contigs, and internal markers have not been tested systematically on these clones. The nature of each marker is indicated below (Ô : PCR marker ; * : hybridization probe ; + : YAC end-probe (hybridization probe)). When available, the genetic position (in cM) of markers on the Recombinant Inbred Lines (RI) map (Lister and Dean, 1997) is indicated. Markers designated "RI" have been mapped on the RI lines (unpublished results from our laboratories) and all apart from AthLecRK were found to be in the correct position relative to flanking markers. Markers designated "atgc" are located on the ATGC RI genetic map. When linkage information is available, although not on the RI map, "CAPS" is noted for CAPS markers and "class" or "int" is noted for markers present respectively on the classical or the integrated map (Hauge et al., 1993).
Solid lines drawn under the markers indicate that the actual order of the markers underlined cannot be determined either from the genetic or physical data. Boxes indicate local inconsistency between the genetic position on the RI map and the position on the YAC map. The extent of each of the 9 contigs is indicated by black solid bars above the markers. An estimated size of each contig is also given (calculated as the total size of the YAC clones for the contig, divided by the mean redundancy observed). Gaps are indicated in grey. The gap between contigs 8.1 and 8.2 (hatched) was filled using the IGF BAC library (look here for details). The gap between contigs 8 and 9 has also been filled using BAC clones (M. Salanoubat, personal communication). The location of the centromere is indicated between contigs 7 and 8. The actual physical sizes of the gaps are unknown, and difficult to estimate from genetic data. The ordered YAC clones are tabulated above the contig line. For each clone, the name is indicated on the left -a "*" indicates that the clone is chimeric-, and the size in kb, as determined by PFGE analysis, on the right (CIC clones : Creusot et al., 1995 ; yUP clones : ATGC web site) . Note that sometimes more than one size is indicated, which means that some yeast clones may contain several YACs, or may actually be a mixture of clones. The size of the lines only reflects the number of markers on a given clone, and by no means gives any indication on the physical size of the clones. Hits with YAC end probes (69) are indicated in dark grey, and other markers in light grey; repetitive DNA probes are hatched. A "-" sign indicates tested markers that are negative on a particular clone, an indication of an internal deletion, and "nt" indicates that the marker was not tested on a particular YAC.
