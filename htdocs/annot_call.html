Call for Experts to Aid in AGI Annotation


The following message, from Larry Parnell (CSHL) and Christine Schueller (MIPS), calls for researchers to volunteer to aid with the annotation of AGI sequence. It was placed on the Arabidopsis newsgroup on Feb. 5, 1998.


We are assembling a list of researchers with expertise in a given gene family or protein group for the purpose of assisting the annotators of the genomic sequence data. It is our desire that these individuals will provide to the annotators their knowledge of a particular gene family, thus resulting in more accurate and richer annotation. This strengthening of ties between the research and genome communities can only benefit both groups.

We anticipate that the annotators will continue to perform the bulk of the work in modeling genes and conducting database searches. However, there are instances when it would be useful to enlist the advice of a colleague. For example, that colleague can impart knowledge of the biological role of the protein and this information can be most useful to the annotator as s/he decides between alternate gene models or in his/her desire to attach that biological information to the sequence.

Please consider lending your services. Our list of experts is publicly available and can be viewed at

http://www.cshl.org/arabweb/genefamilyexperts.htm

Direct questions or comments to:
