Close-Up on Physical Maps

The physical maps show a segment of the chromosome indicated by the numbers at the top of the map. To facilitate more refined positioning of entities displayed, a chromosome ruler is displayed below the chromosome location numbers. The location of the segment within the chromosome is indicated by a red rectangle on the location bar in the map menu:

The Physical maps currently contains three types of entities: Contigs, Probes, and Clones. All displayed entities are hyperlinked to a detail page from the TAIR Database. Each type of entity is drawn above a bar of tick marks. Position of the tick marks indicate the start position of an entity above (indicated by grey arrows in the above diagram). To facilitate the display of multiple maps, a show/hide function was implemented, where only three rows of entities or all of the entities can be shown. Displayed entities and hidden entities are indicated by red or black tick marks, respectively.

See "Show/Hide Entities" for more information.

Below the chromosome are contigs. Each contig shows the positions of probes. Horizontal bars below the probes are clones. Each clone type is colored differently. See "Physical Maps Color Key" for details.

You can view more detailed information about specific clones and probes by clicking on them in the map.
