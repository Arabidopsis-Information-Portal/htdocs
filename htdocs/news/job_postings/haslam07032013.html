Post-Doctoral Research Scientist

Rothamsted Research, the UK's oldest and largest agricultural research centre, is seeking a Post-Doctoral Research Scientist as part of Rothamsted Research's Designing Seeds for Nutrition and Health research programme which aims to utilise our expertise in seed biology and biochemistry to deliver improved health and nutrition through seeds.

The successful applicant will join a well-established oilseed improvement programme, with a specific focus on the role of the lipidome in extreme environments. The contribution to the lipidome (total cellular lipid pool) makes in determining the ability to tolerate suboptimal conditions will be examined at the transcript and metabolite level. The successful candidate will be expected to contribute to the following main research areas:

  • The application of state of the art analytical techniques to identify the key lipids responsible for tolerance of extreme environments and their mechanisms of action
  • Tissue-specific transcriptome sequencing (RNA-Seq) during seed maturation
  • Manipulation of plants using targeted molecular methods

To fill this exciting and challenging role, we are seeking a highly motivated researcher with a proven track record in plant molecular biology and metabolite analysis. Experience of lipid extraction and/or RNA-Seq would be a distinct advantage.

As a member of a multidisciplinary group, the ability to work as part of a team and excellent communication skills are essential.

For further details please contact either Dr Richard Haslam (richard.haslam@rothamsted.ac.uk) or Dr Louise Michaelson (lousie.michaelson@rothamsted.ac.uk).

The post is funded for 3 years at Band D. The appointment is full time with a starting salary normally in the range £28,110 to £31,677 per annum
