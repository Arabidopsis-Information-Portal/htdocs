Abstract

Phenotypic variability and genetic structure of 3 populations growing under different climatic conditions were analyzed in this study. The populations differ in a number of quantitative characters, frequency of embryonic lethals and ratio of spring and winter forms. There is a stable polymorphism in some classes of embryonic lethals. As a result of cross pollination 10 to 20 20 p.c. of the individuals are formed in the populations.

Deswegen sollte A. thaliana unter naturlichen Bedingungen als Pflanze mit fakultativer Kreuzbestaubung betrachtet werden.
