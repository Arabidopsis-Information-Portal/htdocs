INTRODUCTION

Among the great variety of mutagen-induced forms with inheritent variations in plants, the allelic mutations are of particular interest. The determination of a series of multiple alleles for one locus reveals the true genic nature of the arising mutations. Presently it is well known, that allelic mutations differently block the same links in the chain of biological reactions, that is why they may serve as convenient models for studying the genic fine structure and the mechanisms of its action on phenotypic characters.

Having at hand a great collection of chlorophyll mutants of Arabidopsis thaliana, we set ourselves the task to elucidate the allelic interrelations in 8 yellow-green mutants of the chlorina type.

For establishing the allelic interrelations, the functional criterium (test for allelism) based on the fact that at crossing 2 mutants carrying changes in different genes a F 1- hybrid arises, i.e., a diheterozygote having the wild phenote due to the dominance of wildtype alleles of each gene. In such cases it is accepted that the recessive mutations used are complement to each other, i.e., nonallelic. If, however, the crossed mutants carry allelic mutants, in the hybrid the wildtype is not revealed, since both alleles of one and the same gene located in homologous chromosomes carry the mutational changes, i.e., these mutations are not complementary, so-called allelic (Fincham, 1968; Gershkovich, 1983).

RESULTS AND DISCUSSION

The majority of our mutants have the character of point mutations, side by side with the main mutant character (yellow green colour of leaves) they differ both from each other and in comparison with the control by additional quantitative characters (Table 1). These experimental data reveal the pleiotropic character of action of mutations controlling the trait of the chlrorophyll deficiency.

At one locus (58/15) six allelomorphic conditions of one and the same gene re were identified, i.e., crossing the mutants (58/15, V-76, 6.2(14), 12.5.(17), 12.6(15) and F 6 Chl) with each other F 1-generation showed mutant colour. Since mutant 58/15 and V-76 belong to chromosomal linkage group 3, we conclude that allelic mutations can also be classified to the same linkage group.

Summarizing the results of this study we come to the following conclusion. Similar changes in the development of phenotypic character (yellow-green colour of plants) may be determined both by various mutations of different genes and by various mutations in different subunits of one and the same gene. At present we carry out experiments to elucidate "dominant-recessive" interrelations in a group of phenotypically similar allelic mutations.
