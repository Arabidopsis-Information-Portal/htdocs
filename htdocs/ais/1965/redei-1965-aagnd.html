The site of the block in LANGRIDGE's thiamine mutant 1018/6

G. P. REDEI

(Department of Field Crops, College of Agriculture, Columbia, Mo., U.S.A.)

It was reported (LANGRIDGE 1958) that mutant 1018/6 is completely restored by thiamine, and the pyrimidine and the thiazole moieties of the vitamin are ineffective. Recent biochemical studies indicate that the synthesis of thiamine from the specific pyrimidine and the specific thiazole involves several steps (cf. BROWN and REYNOLDS 1963). Thiazole is phosphorylated before it is coupled to pyrimidine. Though LANGRIDGE's mutant responds best to thiamine, 4-methyl-5-beta-hydroxyethyl thiazole alone is also beneficial. It appears therefore that the mutation affects an intermediate step between thiazole synthesis and the coupling of thiazole-monophosphate to pyrimidine-pyrophosphate.
