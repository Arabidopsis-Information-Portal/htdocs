Column Headings for List of Mutants



    

  • Mutant: Name of mutant locus. 
    • 

  • Mutagen: Agent used to induce mutations. 
    • 

  • Eco: Ecotype. COL = Columbia; WS = Wassilewskija. 
    • 

  • Tag: T-DNA tagging status. Y = tagged; N = not tagged; U = unresolved; NAP = not 
applicable. 
    • 

  • Class: Initial classification based on appearance of mutant embryos at maturity. P = 
preglobular; G = globular; T = transition; C = cotyledon; F = fusca; O = other. 
    • 

  • Seed/Embryo: Color of mutant seeds and embryos prior to desiccation. 1 = white; 2 = pale 
yellow-green; 3 = pale green; 4 = green; 5 = green and dark red. 
    • 

  • % m/m: Percent mutant seeds in heterozygous siliques following self-pollination. 
    • 

  • % Top: Percent total mutant seeds located in top half of silique. A non-random (*) 
distribution of mutant seeds suggests that the mutant allele disrupts pollen development or 
pollen-tube growth. 
    • 

  • Linkage: Chromosome assignment based on mapping with visible markers. [ ] = 
assignment based on mapping other mutant alleles; (-) = results pending; (x) = not included 
in mapping project. 
    • 

  • Map: Chromosome location on updated classical genetic map. 
    • 

  • Phenotype: General appearance of mutant embryos at maturity. 
    • 

  • Current: Current gene symbol. 
    • 

  • Source: Original seed source. DuPont and Tucson lines were generated by Ken Feldmann. 
    •
