Applications are invited for a postdoctoral position to study the functional relevance and regulation of alternative splicing in the Plant Molecular Biology group at the Instituto Gulbenkian de Ciência (IGC) near Lisbon, Portugal (http://www.igc.gulbenkian.pt). The position will be initially available for one year, with renewal based on performance and funding availability.

Research in the lab uses Arabidopsis thaliana as a model system and a combination of genetic, molecular/cell biology, biochemical and genomic approaches to investigate how plants respond to environmental cues. Our major line of work is addressing the biological significance of alternative splicing and providing functional evidence linking this key posttranscriptional regulation mechanism to plant abiotic stress responses. Another ongoing project in the lab is uncovering physiological roles for membrane transporters of the Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) in plant responses to different environmental challenges.

We seek creative, highly motivated candidates with the ability to work independently and a strong interest in plant biology. Applicants must hold a PhD with demonstrated expertise in molecular biology, cell biology and biochemistry (publication record).
